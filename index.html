Mystery: What came bofore the big bang?

IT WAS gone almost as soon as it came, so it is hardly surprising that we
didn't even notice it. Only in 20007, six years after the event did Duncan
Lorimer at West Virginia University and his student David Markey.
They were scouring archived data from the Parkes radio telescope located in
South Wales, Australia, when they notices a burst of radio waves approaching with
unimaginable felocity. Lasting less than 5 milliseconds it hit Earth in
20001, releasing roughly as much energy as the sun splits out over five billion years.
